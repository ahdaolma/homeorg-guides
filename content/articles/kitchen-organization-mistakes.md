## 1. Storing Pots and Pans in a Deep Lower Cabinet (Without Pull-Outs)

**The mistake:** Stacking pots and pans in a deep cabinet. You kneel on the floor, reach into darkness, lift a stack of 4 pans to get the one at the bottom. The bottom pan gets used 1/3 as often because it's inaccessible.

**The fix:** A pull-out pot and pan organizer ($30-$50, Lynk or Rev-A-Shelf). Two sliding wire racks that pull out independently. The lower rack holds pots; the upper rack holds pans and lids. Everything is accessible without kneeling. Installation: 4 screws into the cabinet base. 30 minutes. Alternative for renters: a freestanding pot rack ($40) that sits inside the cabinet and slides out.

## 2. Storing Dishes Far From the Dishwasher

**The mistake:** Plates in a cabinet on the opposite wall from the dishwasher. Unloading the dishwasher takes 2x as many steps.

**The fix:** Dishes, glasses, and everyday flatware live in the cabinet CLOSEST to the dishwasher. The 10 feet between the dishwasher and the far cabinet, traversed 365 times per year, equals 1.4 miles of unnecessary walking annually. Rearrange cabinets so the dish zone is adjacent to the dishwasher.

---

## 3. Keeping Small Appliances on the Counter "Because There's No Room in the Cabinets"

**The mistake:** The toaster, coffee maker, blender, stand mixer, air fryer, Instant Pot, and food processor live on the counter. That's 7 small appliances occupying 10+ square feet.

**The fix:** The appliance audit (see Kitchen Counter Clutter article). Keep only daily-use appliances (2-3 max) on the counter. Weekly appliances go to a lower cabinet with a pull-out shelf. Monthly appliances go to a high shelf or pantry.

---

## 4. Storing Food in Original Packaging

**The mistake:** Half-open bags of flour, rice, and cereal in original packaging. You can't see how much is left. Bags spill. Pests get in. Duplicate purchases happen.

**The fix:** Decant all dry goods into clear, airtight containers (OXO POP, $20 each, or Mason jars, $12/dozen). Label with contents AND expiration date on masking tape. FIFO: new product goes in the CONTAINER first, old product on top.

---

## 5. Stacking Lids in a Pile

**The mistake:** Pot lids stacked loose in a cabinet. To get the right lid, you unstack all of them. The pile falls over constantly.

**The fix:** A vertical lid organizer ($10, Lynk) — lids stand on edge like files. One-handed retrieval. Works for pot lids, Tupperware lids, and pan lids.

---

## 6. Ignoring the Inside of Cabinet Doors

**The mistake:** The inside of every cabinet door is blank. That's 8+ square feet of usable vertical storage per kitchen, completely wasted.

**The fix:** Adhesive hooks ($5 for 6, Command) for measuring spoons, pot holders, small cutting boards. An over-the-door organizer ($12) for plastic wrap, foil, parchment paper, and ziplock bags — these are awkward to store in drawers and take up too much drawer space.

---

## 7. The "I'll Organize It Later" Bin

**The mistake:** A bin, bag, or drawer of items "to be organized later." It never gets organized. It becomes permanent clutter with a nicer name.

**The fix:** There is no "later." There is now (deal with the item) or never (donate/trash it). The "organize later" bin is a procrastination tool dressed as an organization system. If you haven't organized the item in the last 30 days, you don't need it.
