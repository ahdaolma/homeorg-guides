## $300 Metal Shelves vs. $100 DIY Wood Shelves — Same Capacity, 3x the Price

Heavy-duty metal garage shelving (Gladiator, Husky, or Muscle Rack) costs $150-$300 per unit and holds 600-800 lbs per shelf. A DIY 2x4 + plywood shelf with the same dimensions (72"W x 24"D x 72"H, 4 shelves) costs $90-$110 in materials and holds 500+ lbs per shelf. The 2-hour build time saves $200. Here's the exact cut list and assembly.

---

## Materials (72x24x72, 4 Shelves)

| Item | Qty | Unit Price | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2x4x8 studs (kiln-dried, not pressure treated — PT warps indoors) | 12 | $4 | $48 |
| 1/2-inch plywood (4x8 sheet), cut into two 2x8 shelf pieces | 2 | $28 | $56 |
| 3-inch #10 wood screws (1 lb box) | 1 | $10 | $10 |
| 2.5-inch #8 wood screws (for plywood) | 1 box | $8 | $8 |
| **Total** | | | **$122** |

Cheaper with OSB instead of plywood ($16/sheet, total $80), but OSB sags under heavy load over time. Plywood is worth the $40 premium for permanent shelving.

---

## Cut List (Have Home Depot Cut the Plywood for Free)

- **4 legs:** 2x4s at 72 inches
- **8 front-to-back supports:** 2x4s at 21 inches (24" depth minus 1.5" for the front leg + 1.5" for the back leg = 21")
- **8 side-to-side rails:** 2x4s at 69 inches (72" width minus 1.5" for left legs + 1.5" for right legs)
- **4 shelf surfaces:** 1/2-inch plywood at 24 x 72 inches (Home Depot makes 2 cuts for free per sheet — have them rip the 4x8 sheet into two 2x8 pieces, then crosscut each at 72")

---

## Assembly (2 Hours)

1. Build two identical side frames (left and right). Each frame: 2 legs (72") connected by 4 horizontal supports (21") — one at the bottom (0"), one at 24", one at 48", and one at 68" (top). Screw through the legs into the supports with two 3-inch screws per joint. Pre-drilling prevents splitting, especially near the ends of 2x4s.
2. Stand both frames upright (get help — they're floppy until connected). Connect them with the 69-inch rails at the front and back of each shelf level. Screw through the legs into the rails. The frame is now rigid.
3. Place each plywood shelf on its support frame. Screw down with 2.5-inch screws every 12 inches around the perimeter. The plywood sits on top of the 2x4 frame — gravity is doing the work. The screws just keep it from shifting.
4. Anchor the shelf to the wall. Garage shelving MUST be secured — a fully loaded shelf is top-heavy. Two L-brackets ($3) screwed into the top rail and into wall studs. If you skip this step, an earthquake, a kid climbing the shelves, or an accidental bump sends 500+ lbs of stored items onto your car or family.

---

## Weight Limits and Spacing

A 21-inch span of 1/2-inch plywood supported on all 4 edges holds approximately 200 lbs distributed. With 3 support rails underneath (front, middle, back — add a third 69-inch rail centered front-to-back), capacity increases to 500+ lbs per shelf by halving the unsupported span. For maximum capacity shelving (storage bins of books, tools, engine parts): add a center support rail to every shelf. The extra $12 in lumber triples capacity.
